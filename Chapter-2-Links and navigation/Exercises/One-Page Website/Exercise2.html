Overview

"House M.D." is a riveting medical drama series that follows the brilliant yet abrasive Dr. Gregory House and his team of diagnosticians as they tackle perplexing medical cases at the Princeton-Plainsboro Teaching Hospital. Created by David Shore, the show's unconventional protagonist, portrayed by Hugh Laurie, navigates the complexities of each case with sharp wit and unorthodox methods, often clashing with hospital authorities and colleagues. Through its compelling blend of medical intrigue, character-driven storytelling, and ethical dilemmas, House M.D. offers a thought-provoking exploration of the human condition, making it a timeless classic in television drama.

Plot

The series revolves around Dr. House's unorthodox methods of diagnosing and treating patients with rare and unusual illnesses. Each episode typically features a new medical mystery and the team's attempts to solve it, often with ethical and personal dilemmas arising along the way.

Cast and Crew

Created by: David Shore
Starring: Hugh Laurie, Lisa Edelstein, Omar Epps, Robert Sean Leonard, Jennifer Morrison
Executive Producers: David Shore, Bryan Singer, Paul Attanasio

Characters

Dr. Gregory House: Brilliant but misanthropic diagnostician with a drug addiction
Dr. Lisa Cuddy: Dean of Medicine and House's boss
Dr. Eric Foreman: Neurologist and member of House's diagnostic team
Dr. James Wilson: House's best friend and head of Oncology
Dr. Allison Cameron: Immunologist and member of House's team

A show worth looking forward to
"I love this show. Fell in love with the pilot, which I got on DVD. I tried watching NBC's Medical Investigation, but it just felt and looked too corny. House is a definite improvement of Medical Investigation. It's smart and daring. I love the sarcasm and snide comments coming from Dr. House. Supporting actors are great. It's CSI-ish but quirky like Arrested Development. I hope it stays on the air. Cancel Medical Investigation but keep House. It's a good sign that FOX is pushing plenty of promos for the show, such as free DVD previews of the pilot. I look forward to watching the show every week. I'd like to see the show develop more of the characters and why Dr. House limps."
- izil, 12/02/2004
